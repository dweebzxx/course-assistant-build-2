## Course Description
<!-- anchor: #course-description -->

We will focus on developing rapid and actionable insights. We will do this by learning to form testable hypotheses, collect relevant data quickly, and perform fundamental analytics. Techniques may include qualitative research, survey design, sample design, online data collection, descriptive statistics, and tests for statistical significance. By the end of class, students will be able to provide convincing recommendations for common marketing decisions.

The course is most appropriate for people who focus on marketing strategy and management. It is also appropriate for people whose job functions involve working with market research and analytics professionals, who need to assess the quality of marketing research, and whose job requires them to work with, interpret, and take action based on data.

---

## Learning Objectives
<!-- anchor: #learning-objectives -->

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. Form testable hypotheses for marketing decisions
2. Collect relevant data quickly and efficiently
3. Perform fundamental analytics including descriptive statistics and tests for statistical significance
4. Conduct qualitative research including observational research, ethnography/netnography, projective techniques, focus groups, and depth interviews
5. Design and execute effective surveys for quantitative research
6. Provide convincing recommendations for common marketing decisions based on data analysis

<!-- anchor: #grading-policy -->

**Grading Components:**

**Grading Criteria Details:**

**Quizzes (25%):**
We will have multiple online quizzes throughout the semester which should be completed individually. The goal of these quizzes is to assess your knowledge of the fundamentals of the course. Due dates are posted on Canvas.

**Group Research Exercises (25%):**
We will have multiple research exercises throughout the semester. The goal of these exercises is to practice course-related skills in a more real-world setting. Assignments and due dates are posted on Canvas. Your grade will be based on your submission. Groups will submit a research plan.

**Group Final Project (30%):**
The final project will be a real-world market research project. You will define and research a marketing problem, propose potential solutions, and execute and evaluate outcomes. Details depend on the project each semester but often involve running qualitative and survey research. Groups will submit a final report and will individually critique one final report.

**Constructive Reviews (20%):**
Individual critiques of peer final reports.

**Graduate Carlson Grading Guidelines:**

Carlson School uses a grading policy based upon a class median aggregate GPA. Learn more about the [Carlson Course Grading Policy](https://z.umn.edu/policy-carlson_graduate_grading).

**Academic Misconduct and Scholastic Dishonesty:**

The Carlson School defines academic misconduct as any act by a student that misrepresents the student's own academic work or that compromises the academic work of another. Scholastic misconduct includes (but is not limited to) cheating on assignments or examinations, plagiarizing, i.e., misrepresenting as one's own work any work done by another, submitting the same paper, or substantially similar papers, to meet the requirement of more than one course without the approval and consent of the instructors concerned, or sabotaging another's work. Within this general definition, however, instructors determine what constitutes academic misconduct in the courses they teach. Students found guilty of academic misconduct face penalties ranging from lowering of the course grade or awarding a grade of F or N for the entire course, to suspension from the University.

Review the [Student Conduct Code](https://z.umn.edu/policy-carlson_student_conduct_code) and [Student Conduct and Academic Integrity website](https://z.umn.edu/student_conduct_academic_integrity).
